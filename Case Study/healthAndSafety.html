The Challenge

One evening, my husband — a Health & Safety officer — shared a recurring frustration: he couldn’t write reports during inspections. Instead, he’d take handwritten notes in the field, then spend hours back at his desk typing them up, uploading photos, and trying to recall exact details. This delay wasn’t just inefficient — it risked losing important context.

That conversation sparked the idea for Inspectra: a tool designed to help safety professionals report incidents on the spot — with photos, location, and notes — all in one simple, mobile-friendly platform.

"Wouldn't it be great", we discussed, "to be able to take my notes right there and?"

Understanding the User

Tackling this issue required a deeper understanding of real-world workflows. I interviewed three Health & Safety officers with varying levels of seniority — from site-level inspectors to senior compliance managers. Each had their own way of working, but all struggled with the same core challenges. James, a junior officer, often juggled multiple inspections in a single day and relied on messy, handwritten notes. Louis, a mid-level safety coordinator, had developed her own spreadsheet system, but found it time-consuming and disconnected from the field. Faysal, a senior HSE manager, emphasized the burden of reviewing late or incomplete reports and the difficulty of maintaining audit readiness.

Synthesizing the most important points and observations (using empathy maps) revealed a few common themes:

Key Insights

  • Report writing is delayed, often leading to lost context or incomplete information.
  • Media and notes are captured separately, making consolidation difficult.
  • Writing reports is time consuming and inconsistent.
  • Superiors lack real-time access to incidents, risking delayed decisions and non-compliance.

User Personas

Theo and David are a HSE agent and a supervisor with different needs.

Theo
"I just want to keep my notes and media captured in the same place."

Theo is a busy HSE agent, who's busy, sometimes wearing gloves and is often under pressure to write reports as soon as the incident happens.

Goals

  • Allow uploading of photos, videos, audio recoringd and location data easily.
  • Be confident that he will not miss any aspect of the incident.
  • Enable quick mobile reporting from field environments.
  • Replace pen-and-paper reporting.

Frustrations

  • Needs to be in office to write the final report
  • Taking hand written notes are hard while working during rain or hot sun
  • Taking pictures using a device then have to send them to his desktop to finish off the report
David
"I need to see reports in realtime with location and media"

David is a health and safety manager who needs to checkup daily on the HSE officer's reports and see what type of actions need to be taken to minimise risks.

Goals

  • Make it easy for managers to receive and organize reports in real time
  • Easily edit existing reports.
  • Check statistics about incident count.

Frustrations

  • A lot of reports without clear categorization
  • Reports only available online
  • Some officers are not tech-savvy, which requires a clean UI

The Solution

To create a solution that's appropriate, trustworthy, and has a place in the market, I developed a unique value proposition that addressed our personas' needs:

Create reports both online and offline
Be able to take media in the realtie and attach it to the report
Have a clear reporting structure with clear categories
Enable quick mobile reporting from the field
Make it easier for managers to check reports and address issues

Problem Statement

Faysal is a health and safety officer who needs to find a way to create incidents reports using his phone directly in the scene without the need to go back to his office.

Goal Statement

The app will allow the users to write offline reports then synced when connected and dispatched to their superiors in an organized way.

Design Process

I started by generating ideas with a "no bad ideas" mindset, sketching solutions addressing persona needs in different ways.

How might we allow users to write reports, share them and allow managers to keep an eye on the scene using clear simple UI?

I created user flows and wireframes to structure the content within the app, focusing on the primary flow of creating and sharing reports.

Final Product

The final design resulted in an intuitive app and a landing page for the desktop version of the platform that allows the user to kick start the reports using mobile phone then finish off (if he wants to) using his desktop.

Key Features

  • HSE agents completed reports quickly
  • Managers could access structured reports instantly, reducing follow-up time
  • Photo attachments reduced back-and-forth by 60%
  • Users rated the interface 4.6/5 for ease of use during testing
